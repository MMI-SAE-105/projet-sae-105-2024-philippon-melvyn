Back to the Flame

Introduction

The book Back to the Flame: The Saga of a Lighter by Linda Perrat, published in 2011, tells the story of an everyday object—the lighter—in an original and humorous way.

In this narrative, the lighter is personified and endowed with human traits, such as intelligence and kindness.

It thus becomes a central character, a keen psychologist and observer of human interactions, navigating through eras with sharp and witty insight.

Synopsis

Through this saga, the lighter "Flame" explores the various situations in which it finds itself involved, evolving alongside humans.

The author plays with temporality, blending a fictional story with a reflection on the evolution of this object, once ubiquitous but now obsolete over time.

This unique approach breathes life into a simple lighter while reflecting on the impact of everyday objects on human life and the disappearance of some of them over time. This original novel offers a mix of fantasy and nostalgia, exploring the significance of objects in our lives and how they influence our collective history, even when they are no longer in use.

Linda Perrat

Linda Perrat is a French author known for her original and humorous approach to storytelling.

She is particularly interested in everyday objects, which she imbues with personality and character.

Perrat uses humor and fantasy to explore deeper themes, such as collective memory and the disappearance of old objects from our lives.
