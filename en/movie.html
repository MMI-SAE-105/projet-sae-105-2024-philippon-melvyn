Movie

The movie The Tinderbox, original title: Fyrtøjet, directed by Svend Methling in 1946, is an adaptation of a Hans Christian Andersen tale.

The story follows a soldier who, after meeting a witch, manages to seize a magical tinderbox.

This tinderbox has the ability to summon dogs that can bring valuable treasures.

Synopsis

As the soldier uses the tinderbox to become wealthy and win the heart of a princess, he also discovers the consequences of his desire for wealth and power.

The story explores themes such as love, ambition, and magic, while adding a touch of romance and fantasy to the tale.

The movie is a captivating story that blends humor and reflection on the choices we make in life, illustrating how ordinary objects can have an extraordinary impact on our destiny.

Svend Methling

A renowned Danish director known for his comedies and popular films, chose to adapt The Tinderbox in 1946, inspired by the famous tale by Hans Christian Andersen, for several reasons.

First, Andersen's work is deeply rooted in Danish culture, and Methling wanted to pay homage to this literary heritage.

Furthermore, the fantastical story of the magical tinderbox provided an opportunity to create a film that was both entertaining and filled with moral lessons about ambition and the consequences of our choices.

Methling, with his sense of humor and love for cinema, found in this tale a way to connect with audiences while exploring universal themes.
