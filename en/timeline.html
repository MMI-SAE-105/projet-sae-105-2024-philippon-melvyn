Timeline

Prehistory - Discovery of Fire 1,000,000 BC

Humanity discovers fire, one of the major inventions in history. Early humans use stones and branches to create sparks.

Antiquity - Use of Flintstone 3000 BC

The Egyptians and Chinese use flintstones, such as flint, to produce sparks and ignite embers.

Middle Ages - Emergence of Spark Lighters 12th Century

Spark lighters appear, often made of metal, with a simple mechanism that produces a spark by striking a piece of steel on a stone.

17th Century - Invention of the Flame Lighter 1610

The flame lighter, using alcohol or other fuels, is invented in Europe and often decorated, considered a luxury item.

19th Century - Gas and Blow Lighters 1823

The invention of the gas lighter allows cleaner and more controlled combustion, with the emergence of blow lighters to stoke the fire.

20th Century - Flint Lighter 1920

The flint lighter, using a flintstone and a liquid fuel reservoir, becomes popular. The Zippo lighter is launched in 1932.

Late 20th Century - Disposable Lighters 1960s

The invention of the disposable lighter, made of plastic, lightweight, and inexpensive, changes the game.

21st Century - Ecological Innovations 2000 and Beyond

The advent of rechargeable, electronic, and eco-friendly lighters using sustainable technologies.
